Narrative

What is Unity? Unity is a collaborative research computing platform that provides academic researchers and industry partners with powerful computing resources and support. It enables users to manage data, conduct high-performance computing tasks, and collaborate efficiently. In this tutorial, I walk through the Unity OnDemand File Browser, demonstrating key storage options and best practices for file management.

What did this project involve? This project involved creating a short, informative video tutorial to help users navigate the Unity OnDemand File Browser. Over the course of two weeks, I worked closely with technical writer Lauren Saloio to translate written documentation into an engaging, accessible video.

Key Skills and Tools Used:

Technical Communication: Adapted complex documentation into an easy-to-follow video format.

Screen Capture & Editing: Used SharePoint Stream and Clipchamp to record and edit the tutorial.

Accessibility & Transcripts: Edited auto-generated transcripts to ensure clarity and accuracy.

Independent Learning & Initiative: Researched additional storage details and best practices beyond the provided documentation.

Collaboration: Engaged with Lauren Saloio and peers to ensure accuracy and completeness.

Key Learnings:

Storage Management: I gained insight into different storage options on Unity and best practices for data persistence.

Video Production: I improved skills in scriptwriting, screen recording, and narration.

Accessibility Considerations: I understood the importance of accurate transcripts and closed captions for inclusive content.

The Importance of Transcripts and Closed Captions

Transcripts and closed captions enhance accessibility by making video content usable for individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing. They also aid comprehension for non-native speakers and improve search-ability. By ensuring my tutorial includes an accurate transcript, I hope to be contributing to a more inclusive learning environment.
