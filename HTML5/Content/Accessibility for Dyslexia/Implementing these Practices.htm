Implementing Dyslexia friendly practices in publishing and data analysis:

Designing accessible content is one thing, but putting those ideas into action is another. This section focuses on the practical tools that publishers and data analysts use every day and how they can be customized to better support readers with dyslexia. From formatting documents in Word to tweaking visualizations in Tableau, small changes in your work flow can lead to significant improvements in user experience.

 

Tips/Tools to aid you in the Publishing Sphere:

Whether you're working in InDesign, Microsoft Word, or exporting PDFs, there are several ways to make your content more dyslexia-friendly:

  • Use dyslexia-friendly fonts like Arial, Verdana, or OpenDyslexic.

  • Left-align text, avoid full justification, and use wide line spacing (1.5x or more).

  • Adjust tracking and leading to create a more precise text flow and ensure sufficient white space.

  • For PDFs, use tagged PDFs to support screen readers and ensure your reading order is logical.

  • When publishing e-books, use platforms that allow for font and background customization (e.g., Kindle or EPUB readers), and offer audiobook or text-to-speech options when possible.

Tips/Tools to aid you in Data Processing:

Data can be overwhelming for individuals with dyslexia, especially when visual elements are cluttered or hard to interpret. These practices help make data tools more accessible:

  • In Tableau or other interactive data software, adjust color schemes to high-contrast palettes and use clear, simple labels.

  • In Excel, enable larger font sizes, use accessible color themes, and add alt text to charts and images.

  • In Power BI, make use of tooltips, accessible visual elements, and the accessibility checker built into the platform.

  • Across all platforms, include alternative text, descriptive labels, and avoid relying on color alone to convey information.

*By embedding these practices into everyday tools, content creators and analysts can ensure their work is inclusive from the ground up.*
